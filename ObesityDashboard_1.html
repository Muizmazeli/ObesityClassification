Key Findings & Recommendations
⚖️
Weight & BMI are Top Predictors
BMI calculated from weight and height accounts for the highest feature importance (0.42) in the classification model. Individuals with BMI > 30 are consistently classified into obesity categories.
  • BMI > 30: High probability of Obesity Type I+
  • BMI 25–30: Overweight category range
  • BMI < 18.5: Insufficient Weight risk
🧬
Family History Strongly Correlates
81% of the dataset reported a family history of overweight. Individuals with family history show 3x higher odds of being classified as Obese Type I or above.
  • Genetic predisposition matters significantly
  • Early screening recommended for at-risk families
  • Preventative lifestyle changes more impactful early
🏃
Physical Activity is Protective
Physical activity frequency (FAF) shows a strong negative correlation with obesity. Individuals with FAF=3 (4-5 days/week) are predominantly Normal Weight or Insufficient Weight.
  • Exercise ≥3 days/week reduces obesity risk by ~60%
  • Sedentary lifestyle (FAF=0) most common in Obese Type III
  • Even light activity (FAF=1) shows measurable benefit
🍽️
Eating Habits Drive Classification
Frequent high-calorie food consumption (FAVC) and eating between meals (CAEC) are the top dietary predictors. Combined with low vegetable frequency, they significantly increase obesity risk.
  • FAVC=yes in 88% of obese individuals
  • CAEC=Always found predominantly in Obesity Type III
  • Increasing vegetables to FCVC=3 shows protective effects
🚗
Transport Mode Matters
Automobile users make up 43% of the dataset and show higher average BMI. Walking and cycling as primary transport correlates with healthier weight categories.
  • Walkers: 70% Normal/Insufficient Weight
  • Automobile users: Higher proportion of Overweight+
  • Active transport = 300-600 extra calories burned/day
💧
Hydration Helps
Water intake (CH2O ≥ 2L/day) correlates with better weight management. Combined with calorie monitoring (SCC=yes), individuals show significantly lower obesity prevalence.
  • CH2O=3 users: 45% less likely to be Obese Type II+
  • Calorie monitoring (SCC) adopted by only 4% — underutilized
  • Water before meals reduces caloric intake effectively
